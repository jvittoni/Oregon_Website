Salem

Oregon's State Capital

Salem is the capital of Oregon. The city has a total area of 48.45 square miles. With being located in the center of Willamette Valley Salem is surrounded by scenic vistas, vineyards, and gardens. There are plenty of places to visit like the Oregon State Capitol, Bush’s Pasture Park, Enchanted Forest, Willamette Heritage Center, and Halie Ford Museum of Art.

  • The city’s population: 177,487 (2022) [1]

  • The year the city was incorporated: 1857 [2]

  • The region of the state in which the city is located: North Central Willamette Valley [3]

  • The classification of the city: urban, suburban, and/or rural: Urban [4]

  • The average income level of the city compared to the rest of the state:
    • City's median household income: $67,540 (2022) [5]
    • City's per capita income: $35,469 (2022) [6]
    • State average income: $64,018 (2021) [7]
